Mutual Aid: Food + Basic Health + Vet Care (No Insurance OK)

This is a starter list for when you’re low on food, money, or access—and need options the system isn’t providing. Pick what fits your situation and your people.

Works offline Shareable Start with zip/city searches

Start here (fastest way to find local help)

  • 211 (United Way) — call/text to get routed to nearby food, rent help, clinics, shelters, etc.
    211.org Find your local 211
  • findhelp (formerly Aunt Bertha) — search free/reduced-cost programs by ZIP code.
    findhelp.org

Food (mutual aid + free meals)

  • Food Not Bombs — volunteer-run free meal shares; many cities have chapters and regular servings.
    foodnotbombs.net
  • Food pantries + community fridges — easiest is usually ZIP-code search via 211 or findhelp.
    Search via 211 Search via findhelp

Medical care without insurance (or low-cost)

  • HRSA Health Centers (FQHCs) — community clinics that provide primary care and often offer sliding-scale fees.
    Find a Health Center HRSA: Get health care
  • Free & Charitable Clinics (NAFC) — search for free/low-cost clinics near you.
    NAFC clinic finder
  • Telehealth (often cheaper than urgent care)
    • Doctor On Demand — 24/7 virtual urgent care + mental health options.
      doctorondemand.com Virtual urgent care
    • Dr. B — low-cost online consults (and a “No-Cost Care” option for people who qualify).
      drb.ai

Medications + medical supplies

  • NeedyMeds — info on prescription assistance programs, discount options, and clinics.
    needymeds.org
  • Ask clinics about “sliding scale” + sample programs — many community clinics can help you access discounted meds, pharmacy assistance, or referrals to programs even if you’re uninsured.
    Find an FQHC Find a free/charitable clinic
  • Local “medical supply closets” / mutual aid supply drives — search via findhelp or 211 using terms like:
    “diapers”, “incontinence supplies”, “wheelchair”, “medical equipment”, “wound care”, “first aid”, “harm reduction”, “naloxone”, “syringe services”.
    Search on findhelp Search via 211

Vet care without insurance

  • Pet Help Finder — search low-cost vets, clinics with discounts/payment options, and pet food/supply help.
    pethelpfinder.org About Pet Help Finder
  • Humane World / Humane Societies — guidance + pointers to low-cost care (often includes spay/neuter & vaccines).
    Humane World resource page
  • ASPCA services — some locations offer low-cost services (availability varies by region).
    ASPCA veterinary services
  • Emergency vet funding (small grants) — if your pet has urgent, life-threatening needs and you’re short on funds:
    RedRover Relief Urgent Care Grants

If you’re overwhelmed

Start with one action:
1) Call 211 and say what you need (food + clinic + meds/supplies + pet care).
2) Search findhelp by ZIP code.
3) If you need care today but can’t access a clinic, consider telehealth options (Doctor On Demand / Dr. B).
