Food (no paperwork, no means testing)

Food Not Bombs

Volunteer-run groups sharing free meals in public spaces. No ID, no sign-up. Often politically active and explicitly anti-capitalist.

foodnotbombs.net

Community Fridges

Public refrigerators stocked by neighbors. Take what you need, leave what you can. Often 24/7.

freedge.org

Healthcare without insurance

HRSA Community Health Centers

Sliding-scale clinics. Primary care, dental, mental health, sometimes meds. Immigration status not required.

findahealthcenter.hrsa.gov

Free & Charitable Clinics

Volunteer clinicians. Often limited hours but truly free care.

nafcclinics.org

Low-cost telehealth

Doctor On Demand

Video visits for urgent care, mental health, and chronic issues. Often cheaper than urgent care, even without insurance.

doctorondemand.com

Doctor B

Asynchronous visits for common conditions (UTIs, birth control, acne, anxiety meds refills). Often **one of the cheapest options**.

doctorb.com

Transport & mobility

Public Transit Access Programs

Some cities offer discounted or free transit passes for low-income residents or people in survival situations. Check your local transit authority.

Mutual Aid Rides

Community volunteers offering rides to medical appointments, grocery trips, or work. Look for local Facebook groups, Slack/Discord, or Nextdoor mutual aid boards.

Bike Share & Free Bikes

Many cities have free or low-cost bike share programs. Local bike co-ops sometimes give away or repair bikes for free.

IDs & documentation

Local ID Clinics

Community organizations that help replace lost or stolen IDs quickly, often for free or sliding-scale. Some also assist with state IDs or driverâ€™s licenses.

Birth Certificates & Vital Records

Nonprofits and legal aid groups can help request copies of birth certificates or other vital records for people without access to mail, bank accounts, or internet.

Social Security Cards

Assistance programs exist for obtaining or replacing Social Security cards, often combined with help for other official documents.

Homeless Documentation Assistance

Programs specifically helping people experiencing housing insecurity obtain necessary IDs to access services, shelters, or healthcare.
